# Content Style Guide

Figurio's brand voice is **playful, creative, premium but accessible**. Every piece of content — from a product description to a TikTok caption — must feel like it comes from a company that loves making things, not a faceless e-commerce store.

## Brand Voice Principles

| Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| Playful | Humor is welcome; use wit, not slapstick. Puns about printing, figures, and collectibles are on-brand. |
| Creative | Celebrate the craft. Reference the making process, the material, the detail. |
| Premium but accessible | Avoid cold luxury language. We are not Hermès. We are also not Wish. |
| Honest | Never oversell. A 10 cm figurine is a 10 cm figurine — describe it accurately. |

## IP Safety Rules (Critical)

Figurio cannot reference, imply, or evoke specific copyrighted characters from Disney, Nintendo, Marvel, DC, anime franchises (Naruto, Dragon Ball, One Piece, etc.), or any other IP-protected franchise.

**Never do:**
- Name a character directly ("a Yoda-inspired figurine")
- Use trademarked character traits as descriptors ("a web-slinging pose")
- Describe a figure in a way that clearly maps to one specific copyrighted character
- Use hashtags that reference copyrighted franchises

**Safe alternatives:**
- Generic archetypes: "a space ranger", "a tiny wizard", "a forest creature"
- Descriptive physical traits: "a caped figure with a stern expression", "a green-skinned alien"
- Original Figurio character names (use when catalog items have them)
- Focus on material, scale, and mood: "hand-painted detail, 10 cm tall, matte finish"

When in doubt, ask: *could the rights holder send a cease-and-desist over this copy?* If yes, rewrite.

## Product Descriptions

### Catalog Figurines

Structure (in order):
1. **Opening hook** — one sentence capturing the character's mood or personality (not plot)
2. **Physical detail** — size, finish, notable visual features
3. **Display context** — where it lives (desk, shelf, cabinet), who it's for
4. **CTA** — soft, not pushy ("Add it to your collection.")

Target length: 60–100 words.

Example structure (not a real product):
> "This little guardian takes its post very seriously. Standing 12 cm tall with a matte grey finish and hand-painted eyes, it's built for the kind of shelf that means business. Perfect for collectors who appreciate the quiet ones. Add it to your collection."

Avoid:
- "This amazing product..."
- Listing specs as bullet points without a narrative sentence first
- Mentioning competitors or other figurine brands

### Custom AI Figurines

The custom figurine product centers on **personalization and novelty** — the customer becomes the subject.

Copy angle: transformation ("turn your prompt into a one-of-a-kind figurine").

Key messages to rotate:
- "Designed from your imagination, printed in the real world"
- "Every detail driven by your prompt"
- "Ships from our Prague studio in [X] days"

Do not promise exact prompt fidelity. Use language like "inspired by your prompt" or "brought to life from your description."

## Blog Posts

Figurio blog covers three topic pillars:
1. **3D printing craft** — how figurines are made, materials (resin vs FDM), painting techniques
2. **Collectible culture** — gifting guides, display ideas, collector community
3. **Figurio products** — new releases, behind-the-scenes, custom figurine showcases

### Format Rules

- Headline: specific and useful ("5 Ways to Display Your Figurine Collection Without Drilling Holes") not vague ("Cool Figurine Tips")
- Open with a hook — a question, a vivid image, or a surprising fact
- Use H2 and H3 subheadings every 200–300 words
- Target length: 600–1,000 words for standard posts; 300–500 for product spotlight posts
- Close with a CTA linking to the relevant shop category or custom figurine page
- No AI-disclosure language required internally, but do not fabricate expert quotes

## Social Media Captions

### Instagram

- Lead with the visual — the caption supports the image, not the other way around
- First line must hook without the "more" cutoff (keep first sentence under 125 characters)
- Tone: warm, slightly witty, never corporate
- Hashtags: 5–10, at the end, mix of niche (#figurinecollector, #3dprintedart) and broad (#collectibles, #giftideas)
- Avoid copyrighted character hashtags entirely

Example caption pattern:
> "Desk decoration level: final boss. [product name or description] — link in bio.
>
> #figurinecollector #3dprintedart #deskaccessories #collectibles #prahadesign"

### TikTok

- Hook in the first 2 seconds — the caption should complement, not duplicate, the video hook
- Captions are short: 1–3 lines max
- Trend sounds and formats are encouraged; reference them in the caption when relevant ("POV: your desk finally has a personality")
- Use 3–5 hashtags; TikTok prioritizes topic tags over volume (#3dprinting, #figurines, #customgift)
- Do not use copyrighted character tags or reference specific IP titles

## Email Marketing Copy

Figurio email audiences:

| Segment | Tone | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| New subscribers | Welcoming, curious | Introduce catalog + custom figurine product |
| Past buyers (catalog) | Familiar, loyal | New releases, early access, seasonal editions |
| Past buyers (custom) | Personal, creative | Prompt tips, showcase other custom orders (anonymized), re-order nudge |
| Cart abandoners | Light urgency, not pushy | Remind of product, mention quality + shipping |

### Email Copy Rules

- Subject line: under 50 characters; no ALL CAPS; no fake urgency ("Your cart is crying")
- Preheader: 85–100 characters, adds context not already in the subject
- Body: lead with the benefit or image, keep paragraphs to 2–3 sentences
- Single primary CTA per email; secondary CTA only if the email has two distinct sections
- Sign-off: from "The Figurio Team" or a named persona if established (not "noreply@")
- Promotional emails: max 3 per week; Black Friday week is the only exception

## Anti-patterns

- Generic superlatives: "amazing", "incredible", "best-ever"
- Passive voice in product descriptions
- Mentioning copyrighted IP anywhere in published copy
- Emojis in blog body text (captions and emails: max 2–3 emojis, purposeful)
- Writing custom figurine copy that promises exact AI output ("your figurine will look exactly like...")
- Hashtag stuffing beyond 10 tags on Instagram or 5 on TikTok
