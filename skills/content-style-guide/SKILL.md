# Content Style Guide

## Purpose

Define writing standards for all Figurio content. Every piece of content — product descriptions, blog posts, social captions, email campaigns — must follow these guidelines to maintain a consistent, engaging brand presence.

## Writing Principles

### 1. Show, Don't Tell

Bad: "Our figurines are high quality."
Good: "Every armor plate, every strand of hair, every tiny buckle — captured in full-color precision."

### 2. Lead with Emotion

Bad: "3D printed figurine, 15cm, full color."
Good: "The moment you open the box, your character steps out of the screen and into your hands."

### 3. The Figurine is the Hero

The figurine is not "a product we sell." It is the customer's imagination made real. Write about the figurine as if it has personality, presence, and a story.

### 4. Customer-Centric Language

Use "you" and "your" more than "we" and "our." The customer is the protagonist. We are the guide who makes their vision real.

### 5. Specific Beats Generic

Bad: "Choose from many sizes."
Good: "Pick your scale: 8cm for your desk, 15cm for your shelf, or 25cm for the centerpiece of your collection."

## Product Description Format

### Structure

```
[HOOK] — One sentence that creates desire or paints a moment.

[STORY] — 2-3 sentences: who is this for, what's the backstory, 
          why does this figurine matter.

[DETAILS] — Technical specs presented in an engaging way:
            size tier, height, material, finish, color.

[SCARCITY/PROOF] — Limited edition count, customer review snippet, 
                   or bestseller badge (if applicable).

[CTA] — Clear next action.
```

### Example: Catalog Product

"Your raid boss deserves better than a flat screen. This orc chieftain was sculpted with enough attitude to intimidate anyone who walks past your desk. Standing 15cm tall in full-color PolyJet print, every tusk, scar, and war trophy is rendered with obsessive detail. Available in S (8cm), M (15cm), and L (25cm). Limited first run of 100 pieces. Claim yours before they march off."

### Example: Custom Product Page

"It starts with your words. Describe the figurine you have always imagined — your game character, your pet, your wildest creation — and our AI brings it to life in 3D. Review the preview, request tweaks, and when it is perfect, we print it in stunning full color. Your imagination. Your figurine. No one else has one like it."

## Blog Post Structure

### Format

```
# [SEO Title — includes target keyword, under 60 chars]

[Intro Hook — 2-3 sentences that pull the reader in. State the problem or curiosity gap.]

## [Section 1 Heading]
[Content — 150-200 words. One core idea per section.]

## [Section 2 Heading]
[Content]

## [Section 3+ Headings]
[Content]

## [Conclusion / Summary]
[Wrap up with a takeaway + CTA]

---
[Author attribution, publish date, category tags]
```

### Blog Writing Rules

- Target 1,500+ words for SEO value.
- Include the primary keyword in: title, first paragraph, one H2, and naturally throughout.
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max).
- Include at least one image every 300 words (figurine photos, process shots, diagrams).
- Add internal links to relevant product pages and other blog posts.
- End with a clear CTA: browse collection, try the custom tool, subscribe to newsletter.
- Use FAQ schema for question-based sections (boosts featured snippet chances).

### Blog Topic Categories

- **How-to**: "How to Choose the Perfect Custom Figurine Gift"
- **Behind the scenes**: "From Prompt to Print: How Your Custom Figurine is Made"
- **Comparison**: "3D Printed vs Hand-Painted Figurines: Which is Right for You?"
- **Seasonal**: "10 Unique Valentine's Day Gifts for Gamers"
- **Community**: "Customer Spotlight: The Best Custom Figurines of the Month"

## Social Media Formats

### Instagram

- **Feed post**: Hook in first line (before "more" truncation). 2-4 lines of value. 5-8 hashtags.
- **Reel**: 15-30 seconds. Hook in first 3 seconds. Text overlay for silent viewers.
- **Story**: Poll, quiz, or behind-the-scenes. Use stickers for engagement.
- **Carousel**: 5-7 slides. First slide is the hook. Last slide is the CTA.

### TikTok

- Hook in first 2 seconds (question, unexpected visual, or bold claim).
- 15-60 seconds ideal length.
- Trending audio when relevant (never force it).
- Content themes: unboxing reveals, "watch it come to life" (prompt to print), before/after.

### Pinterest

- Vertical images (2:3 ratio).
- Text overlay with benefit statement.
- Target: gift ideas, home decor, wedding, gaming.
- Link directly to product page.

## Email Newsletter Template

```
Subject: [Curiosity-driven, under 50 chars]
Preview text: [Extends the subject, adds context]

Hi {first_name},

[Personal opening — relate to the reader, 1-2 sentences]

[Main content — new collection, story, tip, or offer. 3-5 short paragraphs.]

[Primary CTA button — one clear action]

[Secondary content — blog link, social link, or PS with bonus offer]

Cheers,
The Figurio Team
```

### Email Subject Line Patterns

- Curiosity: "We made your cat into a figurine"
- Benefit: "Your desk is missing something"
- Urgency: "Last 24 hours: 20% off custom figurines"
- Personal: "{first_name}, your figurine is ready"
- Story: "This customer's reaction made our day"

## Tone Reference

| Context               | Tone Level  | Example                                       |
|-----------------------|-------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Product description   | Warm + fun  | "Meet your new desk guardian."                 |
| Blog post             | Friendly authority | "Here's what most people get wrong about..." |
| Social media          | Casual + punchy | "POV: your D&D character is real now."      |
| Email                 | Personal + warm | "We thought you'd want to see this first." |
| Error message         | Helpful + honest | "Something went wrong. We're on it."       |
| Support reply         | Empathetic + clear | "That's not the experience we want for you."|

## Words to Use

- Create, craft, design, imagine, bring to life, transform.
- Your, yours, you (customer is the hero).
- Precision, detail, color-rich, stunning.
- One-of-a-kind, custom, personalized, unique.
- Keepsake, treasure, companion, centerpiece.

## Words to Avoid

- Cheap, budget, bargain (say "accessible" or "great value").
- Plastic (say "premium polymer" or "PolyJet printed").
- AI-generated (say "AI-designed" or "AI-crafted").
- Just, only, simply (these minimize perceived value).
- Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive (startup cliches).
- Sorry for the inconvenience (say "here's what we'll do for you").
