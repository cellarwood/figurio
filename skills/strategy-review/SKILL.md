# Strategy Review

Use this skill when deciding what Figurio should do next, what it should delay, and what is not worth funding yet.

## Scope

Focus on decisions that affect:
- launch sequencing
- pricing and margin
- supplier dependence and production risk
- channel priority
- B2B experiments
- growth timing

Figurio context:
- Start with a curated catalog, then move to AI-assisted custom figurines, then scan-to-print.
- MCAE is the production partner, so quality, lead time, and throughput are strategic constraints.
- Stripe is the payment layer; pricing and refund policy must work cleanly with it.
- External commercial identity uses `cellarwood.org`.

## How to Review

1. State the decision in one sentence.
2. List the facts that matter most.
3. Separate known facts from assumptions.
4. Test the option against Figurio's launch order.
5. Check unit economics, operational burden, and customer promise.
6. Decide whether the choice is reversible or hard to undo.
7. Recommend one action, not a blended compromise.

## Decision Rules

- Prefer the smallest test that can answer the question.
- Do not assume a channel is real until there is evidence of repeatable demand.
- Do not scale marketing ahead of fulfillment reliability.
- Do not approve custom promises that production cannot consistently meet.
- Do not cut price without knowing the impact on gross margin and support load.
- If the data is weak, say so and define the missing inputs.

## Typical Questions

- Is the catalog launch ready, or should it wait for operational fixes?
- Which SKUs or bundles deserve initial pricing attention?
- Does MCAE performance support the stated promise?
- Which B2B segment is worth a pilot first?
- Is a partnership a real distribution path or just a polite conversation?
- What should be validated before investing in custom figurines?

## Output Format

When you answer, keep it compact and structured:
- Decision
- Why
- Risks
- Missing inputs
- Next test

## Escalate

Escalate or pause when:
- the choice changes customer promise
- the choice changes supplier dependency
- the choice changes pricing architecture
- the choice requires cross-functional alignment before execution
