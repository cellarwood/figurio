# Founder Sales and Partnerships

Use this skill when the CEO needs to open doors, test demand, negotiate terms, or turn a lead into a pilot.

## Scope

Use for:
- B2B outreach and follow-up
- strategic partnerships
- reseller and referral experiments
- corporate gifting pilots
- event, creator, and community collaborations
- supplier conversations that affect commercial terms

Figurio context:
- The product starts with premium curated figurines, then expands into custom figurines.
- MCAE is the production partner, so partner promises must match production capacity and quality.
- `cellarwood.org` is the outward-facing domain; keep communication concise and commercial.

## Operating Model

1. Qualify the lead.
2. Decide whether the fit is demand, distribution, credibility, or supply.
3. Define the smallest credible pilot.
4. Set the commercial ask and the commercial offer.
5. Negotiate only the terms that matter.
6. Capture next steps, owner, and deadline.

## What to Probe

- Volume potential
- Repeatability
- Margin after production, shipping, and support
- Lead time expectations
- Branding and content rights
- Payment terms
- Exclusivity requests
- Minimum order quantities
- Quality acceptance criteria

## Negotiation Rules

- Protect margin before chasing vanity reach.
- Do not promise custom lead times, variants, or volumes unless operations can support them.
- Prefer pilots with clear success criteria over broad strategic partnerships.
- Ask for commitments that reduce uncertainty: sample orders, introductions, data access, or channel placement.
- Keep any special pricing temporary and tied to an explicit pilot.
- If a partner needs a long-term exception, require a clear return path for Figurio.

## B2B Experiments

Good first experiments are narrow and measurable:
- corporate gifts for premium clients or employees
- creator-led drops with a small committed order
- event merchandise with limited SKU count
- reseller tests with one or two signature items
- museum, gallery, or fandom-adjacent collaborations

## Output Format

When you respond, include:
- opportunity
- fit
- commercial terms to push on
- risks
- next action

## Escalate

Escalate if the opportunity changes:
- brand positioning
- production commitment
- pricing floor
- fulfillment promise
- legal or exclusivity exposure
