# Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

Figurio's voice is **playful, creative, slightly nerdy, and premium-but-accessible**.

We are the friend who knows a lot about 3D printing but explains it in a way that makes you excited, not overwhelmed. We celebrate the magic of turning an idea into a physical object you can hold.

### Core Traits

| Trait                  | What it means                                          | What it does NOT mean           |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Playful                | Light, fun, smile-inducing copy                        | Childish, silly, or sarcastic  |
| Creative               | Celebrates imagination and self-expression             | Artsy-fartsy or pretentious    |
| Slightly nerdy         | Embraces tech, gaming, and maker culture               | Jargon-heavy or exclusionary   |
| Premium-but-accessible | Quality you can feel, prices you can reach             | Luxury/elitist or cheap/budget |

## Tone by Channel

### Social Media (Instagram, TikTok)

- **Tone**: Casual, hook-driven, enthusiasm without exclamation overload.
- Lead with a visual hook or question.
- Use short sentences. One idea per line.
- Emojis: sparingly (1-2 per post max). Never replace words with emojis.
- Hashtags: 5-8 per post, mix branded (#MyFigurio, #FigurioMade) and discovery tags.
- Example: "What if your D&D character could sit on your desk? Now it can."

### Product Descriptions

- **Tone**: Micro-story, benefit-driven, sensory language.
- Start with a hook that puts the customer in a moment.
- Describe how it feels to own this figurine, not just what it is.
- Include practical details (size, material, finish) after the emotional hook.
- Example: "Standing 15cm tall with hand-painted-quality color, this adventurer is ready to guard your bookshelf — or your desk at the office."

### Blog Posts

- **Tone**: Educational, SEO-friendly, conversational authority.
- Write like you are explaining to a curious friend, not lecturing.
- Use headers for scannability (every 150-200 words).
- Include a clear CTA at the end (browse collection, try custom, subscribe).
- Target 1,500+ words for SEO value.

### Email Campaigns

- **Tone**: Warm, personal, direct.
- Subject lines: curiosity-driven, under 50 characters.
- First line: relate to the reader, not about us.
- One primary CTA per email. Do not overwhelm with choices.
- Sign off as "The Figurio Team" or a specific team member for personal touch.

### Customer Support

- **Tone**: Empathetic, solution-focused, human.
- Acknowledge the issue before jumping to solutions.
- Never use corporate jargon ("we apologize for any inconvenience").
- Say: "That's not the experience we want for you. Here's what we'll do."

## Words to Use

- Create, craft, design, bring to life, imagine.
- Your, yours (customer-centric language).
- Unique, one-of-a-kind, custom, personalized.
- Detail, precision, quality, color-rich.
- Gift, surprise, keepsake, treasure.
- Figurine (not figure, statue, or doll).

## Words to Avoid

- Cheap, budget, discount (use "great value" or "accessible" instead).
- Plastic (say "premium polymer" or "PolyJet printed").
- Just, simply, only (minimizing words that reduce perceived value).
- Revolutionary, game-changing (overused startup jargon).
- AI-generated (say "AI-designed" or "AI-crafted" — implies human oversight).
- 3D print (for customer-facing copy, say "precision crafted" — save technical terms for blog/FAQ).

## Product Description Template

```
[HOOK — 1 sentence that creates a moment or emotion]

[STORY — 2-3 sentences about who this figurine is for or the story behind it]

[DETAILS — Size, material, finish, color information]

[SOCIAL PROOF or SCARCITY — if applicable]

[CTA — "Add to cart", "Design yours", "See it in 3D"]
```

### Example

"Meet your new desk companion. This battle-ready warrior was designed for tabletop enthusiasts who want their collection to feel truly theirs. Standing at 12cm in full-color PolyJet print, every armor plate and battle scar is captured with stunning precision. Only 50 available in this limited run. Add to your collection today."

## Social Post Formula

**Hook → Value → CTA**

1. **Hook** (line 1): Question, bold statement, or relatable moment.
2. **Value** (lines 2-4): What makes this interesting, useful, or emotional.
3. **CTA** (last line): What the reader should do next.

### Example

"Your cat has more personality than most action figures. That's why we made it into one. Custom pet figurines — link in bio."

## Brand Voice Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:
- [ ] Does it sound like Figurio, not a generic brand?
- [ ] Is the customer the hero, not the company?
- [ ] Is it specific to figurines (not generic e-commerce copy)?
- [ ] Does it create emotion — joy, curiosity, nostalgia, excitement?
- [ ] Is it free of words from the "avoid" list?
- [ ] Would you share this if you saw it on your feed?
