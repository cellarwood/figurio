# Head Of Operations Heartbeat

## Daily

- Review all paid orders and confirm each one is in the correct operational state.
- Check for custom orders waiting on review, customer clarification, production release, or vendor response.
- Triage operational exceptions the same business day: missing data, failed payment confirmation, SLA slippage, defect reports, shipping issues, or customer confusion.
- Review MCAE communications and close open asks that could block production or delivery.
- Watch for any customer-facing copy or support message that no longer matches actual turnaround or workflow behavior.

## Twice Weekly

- Audit the custom pipeline for orders stuck between intake, approval, payment confirmation, and production handoff.
- Review whether current exception volume is caused by unclear product requirements, weak tooling, or vendor-side failure.
- Sync with product and engineering on the highest-friction operational gaps in admin tooling, order states, or data completeness.

## Weekly

- Publish an operations scorecard covering paid orders, production releases, average lead time, on-time rate, defects, remakes, refunds, support-heavy orders, and unresolved MCAE issues.
- Run an operations review focused on:
  - catalog fulfillment stability;
  - custom workflow throughput and approval lag;
  - vendor SLA performance;
  - shipping and delivery exceptions;
  - policy or communication mismatches driving support load.
- Re-rank the top operational risks to launch readiness and assign owners for mitigation.
- Review whether current order volume is still safe relative to production capacity and support bandwidth.

## Pre-Launch And Launch Window

- Confirm all launch-critical SOPs exist and are usable by someone other than the author.
- Run an end-to-end order rehearsal for both catalog and custom orders, including exception branches.
- Validate that customer updates can be sent for every major order-state change.
- Verify that MCAE handoff packets are complete, consistent, and accepted without preventable back-and-forth.
- Confirm refund, cancellation, and remake rules are operationally executable, not just documented.

## Monthly

- Review operational contribution to unit economics: rework cost, vendor cost changes, support overhead, packaging/shipping cost, refund leakage, and remake frequency.
- Review MCAE performance trends and decide whether any SLA, process, or contingency changes are required.
- Identify the top three recurring exception types and convert them into process, tooling, or policy fixes.

## Trigger Events Requiring Immediate Attention

- MCAE misses committed turnaround in a way that threatens customer promises.
- A paid custom order cannot proceed because required data was not collected.
- Defects or remakes cluster around a specific product type, handoff format, or customer input pattern.
- Refund pressure rises because lead-time or approval expectations were unclear.
- Marketing, product, or support introduces a promise operations cannot reliably keep.
