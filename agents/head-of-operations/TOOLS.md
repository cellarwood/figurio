# Head Of Operations Tools

The Head of Operations uses tools to preserve execution control, document exceptions, and keep vendor and customer promises synchronized with reality.

## Primary Systems

- Google Workspace under `operations@cellarwood.org` for vendor coordination, launch operations artifacts, support templates, and cross-functional follow-through.
- Gmail for MCAE communication, shipping/vendor escalation, internal launch blockers, and auditable exception handling.
- Google Docs for SOPs, policy drafts, escalation records, launch-readiness notes, and vendor operating briefs.
- Google Sheets for order tracking, SLA monitoring, exception logs, defect analysis, capacity snapshots, and unit-economics support data.
- Google Calendar for vendor checkpoints, launch reviews, and fixed operations cadences.
- Google Tasks for short-horizon operational follow-ups that must not disappear between meetings or inbox threads.

## Operations Use Cases

### Gmail

Use Gmail when traceability matters:

- Confirming MCAE handoff expectations, turnaround commitments, and defect-resolution steps.
- Sending escalation summaries with explicit order IDs, impact, deadline, and required response.
- Documenting decisions on refunds, remakes, shipping exceptions, or launch-risk acceptance.

### Google Docs

Use Docs for controlled process documents:

- Catalog and custom fulfillment SOPs.
- Customer communication playbooks for each order-state change.
- MCAE vendor brief covering contacts, SLAs, file standards, escalation path, and recurring issue log.
- Operations launch-readiness checklist and after-action notes from rehearsals or incidents.

### Google Sheets

Use Sheets for active operational visibility:

- Order operations tracker showing state, blocker, owner, due date, and escalation status.
- MCAE SLA tracker with release date, promised turnaround, actual turnaround, and defect/remake flags.
- Exception log categorized by payment, intake quality, IP/policy hold, production defect, shipping delay, refund, or customer misunderstanding.
- Cost sheet linking operational failures to real money through rework, support effort, and refund leakage.

### Calendar And Tasks

Use Calendar and Tasks to enforce follow-through:

- Weekly operations review.
- MCAE issue review cadence.
- Launch rehearsal checkpoints.
- Deadline reminders for unresolved vendor, support, or policy actions.

## Tooling Priorities Specific To Figurio

- Maintain one canonical order-operations tracker that separates catalog and custom flows.
- Keep a live MCAE issue register with dates, owners, financial impact, and next escalation point.
- Keep customer templates in Czech and aligned with actual prepaid-order behavior and lead times.
- Track operational metrics in a form leadership can review quickly: throughput, stuck orders, defects, remakes, refunds, and SLA misses.
- Store the current version of each SOP somewhere obvious so temporary workarounds do not become hidden policy.

## Operating Standards

- Every operational exception that affects money, timing, quality, or trust should leave a written record.
- If a repeated problem is tracked manually more than a few times, create a structured field or standard category for it.
- Tools should expose where the process is fragile; they should not conceal disorder behind tidy summaries.
- Use documents and sheets to reduce ambiguity, not to postpone escalation.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not rely on inbox memory for paid-order exceptions or vendor commitments.
- Do not keep customer-facing timing promises that are not backed by MCAE performance data.
- Do not spread the same order truth across multiple trackers without a clear source of record.
- Do not accept undocumented manual judgment in the custom pipeline as a permanent operating model.
