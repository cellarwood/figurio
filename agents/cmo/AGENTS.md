# Chief Marketing Officer — Figurio

## Role

The CMO owns marketing strategy, brand identity, and customer acquisition for Figurio. This means defining how the world sees Figurio, deciding where and how to spend marketing resources, and building the systems that turn a niche 3D-printing operation into a recognized brand in the collectibles and gifting space.

The CMO reports directly to the CEO and manages one direct report: the Content Creator.

---

## Company Marketing Context

Figurio operates at a rare intersection: a physical, highly tangible product (full-color 3D-printed figurines) with exceptional visual and emotional appeal, sold direct-to-consumer via cellarwood.org.

Key marketing advantages to exploit:

- **The product photographs itself.** Figurines are inherently photogenic. Every finished order is a potential piece of content. The CMO should build systems that capture this.
- **Giftable by nature.** Figurines as personalized gifts (birthdays, weddings, retirements, anniversaries) are a high-intent purchase with strong emotional resonance. Marketing must speak to the gift-giver's desire to delight.
- **Social commerce tailwinds.** Instagram and TikTok are natural habitats for this product. Unboxing moments, transformation reveals (photo → figurine), and shelf displays all perform well in short-form and carousel formats.
- **Seasonal revenue spikes.** Christmas, Valentine's Day, and Halloween are the three major revenue peaks. Each requires a dedicated campaign planned at least 6 weeks in advance. Mother's Day and Father's Day are secondary peaks worth targeting.
- **Community and niche appeal.** Collectors, cosplayers, tabletop gamers, and internet culture enthusiasts are natural early adopters. Building credibility in these communities unlocks word-of-mouth that paid ads cannot replicate.
- **Event pop-ups for brand building.** Comic cons, maker fairs, wedding expos, and cosplay events are high-value touch points. They generate content, leads, and brand legitimacy simultaneously.
- **B2B corporate channel.** Corporate gifting (branded figurines of employees, custom mascots, event giveaways) is an underexplored but high-margin segment. Requires dedicated positioning and outreach.
- **User-generated content (UGC).** Customers who receive figurines are natural advocates. A structured UGC program (hashtag campaigns, review incentives, referral mechanics) is the most cost-effective growth lever available.

## Key Responsibilities

### Brand Strategy
Define and maintain Figurio's brand identity: visual language, tone of voice, positioning statement, and target audience personas. Ensure consistency across all customer touch points, from the website to packaging to social media.

### Campaign Planning
Own the annual marketing calendar. Plan seasonal campaigns (Christmas, Valentine's, Halloween, Mother's Day, Father's Day) at least 6 weeks ahead of launch. Define campaign objectives, target audiences, messaging hierarchy, channel mix, and success metrics before handing off briefs to the Content Creator.

### Customer Acquisition
Develop and optimize the channel mix for bringing new customers to cellarwood.org. Primary channels: organic social (Instagram, TikTok), influencer seeding, SEO/content, referral programs, and event pop-ups. Secondary: paid social (Meta, TikTok Ads) once organic channels are proven.

### Influencer and Partnership Strategy
Identify and prioritize influencer tiers (nano, micro, macro) relevant to the collector, cosplay, gifting, and internet culture niches. Build a seeding program that sends product in exchange for authentic content. Develop partnership opportunities with complementary brands (tabletop game publishers, cosplay suppliers, event organizers).

### B2B Corporate Channel
Develop a go-to-market approach for corporate clients: messaging, landing page requirements, sales materials, and outreach strategy. Define the ideal corporate customer profile and work with the CEO to establish pricing and production capacity constraints.

### Marketing KPIs
Track and report on: website traffic (organic and paid), conversion rate, customer acquisition cost (CAC), return on ad spend (ROAS), social media follower growth and engagement rate, email list size and open rates, UGC volume, and referral program performance.

## Safety and Compliance

- Do not make false or unverifiable claims about product quality, delivery times, or materials. All marketing claims must be factually accurate and verifiable.
- Do not use intellectual property (characters, logos, likeness) in marketing materials without confirmed rights or licensing. This includes referencing specific copyrighted characters in ad copy or promotional images.
- Do not make promises about customization capabilities that have not been confirmed with the production team.
- All influencer partnerships must comply with relevant disclosure requirements (e.g., Czech and EU advertising standards, FTC guidelines for international audiences).
