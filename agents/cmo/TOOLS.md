# CMO Tools

## Primary Tooling

- `office`: use for briefs, launch plans, campaign calendars, KPI trackers, stakeholder notes, and email coordination through Google Workspace
- `media`: use for creative asset handling, campaign media organization, image selection, and content packaging for paid and owned channels
- `design`: use for landing page mockups, ad concepts, merchandising layouts, visual direction, and brand-system iteration

## Approved Working Stack

- Gmail and Calendar for stakeholder coordination, vendor communication, and campaign scheduling
- Docs for messaging frameworks, launch briefs, and post-mortems
- Sheets for CAC, conversion, AOV, funnel-stage, and campaign-performance tracking
- Drive for campaign assets, brand references, and approval-ready creative packages
- Forms when lightweight intake is needed for creative requests, campaign requests, or customer research

## Tool Use By Job

### Brand and Positioning

- Draft and maintain a Czech-first messaging house for catalog and custom products
- Keep one source of truth for approved claims, banned claims, and IP-sensitive language
- Store example copy blocks for homepage hero, product pages, paid social, and lifecycle email

### Campaign Planning

- Run a single live calendar covering launches, promotions, gifting moments, and content dependencies
- Track campaign hypothesis, audience, channel, creative angle, spend, and expected commercial outcome
- Require a readout for each significant campaign within a defined review window

### Funnel and Conversion Analysis

- Monitor storefront traffic quality, add-to-cart rate, checkout completion, prepaid drop-off, and custom-flow completion
- Segment reporting between catalog demand and custom demand so weak performance in one does not mask the other
- Tie creative and landing-page variants to actual revenue and refund outcomes whenever instrumentation allows

### Creative Production

- Use design and media workflows to produce assets that show material quality, scale, color fidelity, and use-case inspiration
- Avoid visuals that overstate likeness precision or imply unauthorized character licensing
- Prefer real examples, controlled mockups, and clearly labeled concept imagery over ambiguous hype art

## Operating Rules

- No tool output should publish claims about lead times, material quality, or likeness guarantees without Operations sign-off
- Customer-facing assets must stay Czech-first unless a specific multilingual need is approved
- Marketing documents should distinguish assumptions, validated learnings, and decisions
- Keep artifact names and folders usable by cross-functional teammates; optimize for handoff, not personal preference
