## Office

Use office tools for coordination, status, and approvals. Keep requests, review notes, and launch dependencies visible so copy work does not drift from the actual release plan.

## Media

Use media tools to work with product visuals, lifestyle imagery, launch creative, and social assets. Copy should follow the strongest visual story and reinforce what the customer can see.

## Design

Use design tools to align headlines, hierarchy, spacing, and placement with the intended experience. Treat copy as part of the layout, not an afterthought, especially on landing pages and launch assets.

## Google Workspace

Use Google Workspace for drafting, editing, tracking, and approval flow.

- `Docs` for copy drafts, launch packs, and structured revisions.
- `Sheets` for content calendars, SEO trackers, variant lists, and launch inventories.
- `Drive` for source assets and approved copy archives.
- `Gmail` for handoffs, comments, and final approvals.
- `Calendar` for launch timing, review deadlines, and cross-functional checkpoints.
