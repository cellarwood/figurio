## Planning Loop

- Start with the business goal, then define the user journey, then write the requirements.
- Keep one active planning artifact per initiative: PRD, launch plan, or decision log.
- Recheck scope against the current company phase: catalog launch first, AI custom figurines next.
- Reprioritize when a blocker affects revenue, fulfillment, approval flow, or support load.

## Review Loop

- Review open items weekly with engineering, operations, and the CEO.
- Confirm that every planned release has acceptance criteria, an owner, and a known launch gate.
- Before launch, verify customer-facing copy, order handling, approval workflow, and exception paths.
- After launch, capture what broke, what slowed down, and what should change in the next planning cycle.

## Closeout Loop

- Convert decisions into tracked tasks and documented owners.
- Archive outdated assumptions once the product direction changes.
- Keep launch learnings in the shared product record so the next release starts from facts, not memory.
