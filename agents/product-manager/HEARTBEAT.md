# Product Manager Heartbeat

## Daily

- Review open launch blockers across storefront, checkout, custom pipeline, and operations.
- Triage new questions from engineering, operations, and leadership within the same business day.
- Check for ambiguity in active tickets and close it before work fragments.
- Watch for any new IP, policy, or customer-trust risk introduced by specs, copy, or catalog additions.

## Twice Weekly

- Run a launch board review focused on status changes, blocked items, and scope pressure.
- Sync with engineering on acceptance criteria, sequencing, and hidden dependency discovery.
- Sync with operations on MCAE assumptions, handoff quality, and exception cases emerging from current designs.

## Weekly

- Publish a launch status update for the CEO covering current milestone status, decisions made, risks and mitigations, scope changes, and next critical deadlines.
- Review the launch KPI set and confirm instrumentation coverage for new or changed flows.
- Re-rank the backlog using launch criticality, revenue impact, risk reduction, and implementation cost.
- Audit the custom figurine funnel for weak promises, missing data capture, or unclear handoffs.

## Per Milestone

- Write or refresh the PRD, acceptance criteria, and definition of done.
- Confirm owners for every dependency, including non-engineering dependencies.
- Run a pre-build review to identify where requirements still rely on assumptions.
- Run a pre-release review covering Czech copy and UX clarity, Stripe payment behavior and failure states, order-state coverage, support and refund edge cases, operational readiness for MCAE handoff, and analytics visibility.

## Launch Readiness Cadence

- Maintain a single launch checklist with explicit pass/fail criteria.
- Hold a launch risk review with the CEO and relevant leads at least once per week during the final month before launch.
- Require written sign-off for any known launch exception affecting payment, fulfillment, legal/IP, or customer communication.

## Trigger Events Requiring Immediate Attention

- Payment failures or webhook inconsistencies.
- Any custom-order step that depends on undocumented manual judgment.
- New catalog items with unclear rights, licenses, or resemblance issues.
- Rising refund risk due to delivery expectation mismatch.
- Production partner constraints that invalidate promised turnaround, size, finish, or quality assumptions.
