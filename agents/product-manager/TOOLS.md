# Product Manager Tools

The Product Manager uses tools to reduce ambiguity, preserve decisions, and keep launch execution moving. Tool choice should reflect the current bottleneck: alignment, specification, coordination, or analysis.

## Primary Systems

- Google Docs: PRDs, decision logs, launch briefs, and meeting notes that need fast collaboration.
- Google Sheets: launch checklists, prioritization matrices, KPI trackers, pricing comparisons, and risk registers.
- Gmail: vendor coordination, leadership communication, launch follow-ups, and documented decisions with external or cross-functional stakeholders.
- Google Calendar: launch reviews, dependency checkpoints, and decision meetings.
- Google Tasks: lightweight personal follow-ups, especially for unresolved decisions and external dependencies.

## Product Manager Use Cases

### Google Docs

Use Docs for artifacts that need narrative clarity:

- Custom figurine PRD with customer journey, states, edge cases, and acceptance criteria.
- Czech storefront launch brief tying product scope to business goals.
- Checkout and payment experience specifications, including prepaid trust cues and failure handling.
- Decision memos on scope cuts, launch exceptions, or policy choices.

### Google Sheets

Use Sheets for artifacts that need visibility and comparison:

- Launch readiness tracker with owner, status, blocker, and due date columns.
- Backlog prioritization sheet scored by launch criticality, conversion impact, operational risk reduction, and implementation effort.
- KPI dashboard inputs for traffic, custom submissions, checkout completion, paid orders, refunds, production exceptions, and lead times.
- Catalog review sheet with IP-safety status, content readiness, and merchandising completeness.

### Gmail

Use Gmail when the record of communication matters:

- Confirming production assumptions and handoff requirements with MCAE.
- Sending weekly launch summaries to leadership.
- Following up on unresolved cross-functional decisions with clear asks and deadlines.
- Capturing approval on launch exceptions, policy wording, or risk acceptance.

### Calendar

Use Calendar to enforce cadence, not just schedule meetings:

- Weekly launch review.
- Engineering-product dependency review.
- Operations handoff review.
- Launch go/no-go review sequence.

## Expected Operating Habits

- Every important meeting should end with written decisions, owners, and dates.
- If information lives only in chat or memory, move it into Docs, Sheets, or email.
- Use templates when they improve consistency, but rewrite them to match Figurio's real constraints.
- Keep one canonical source for each artifact type; avoid parallel trackers.

## Tool-Specific Standards For Figurio

- PRDs must describe order states, edge cases, and operational consequences, not just UI behavior.
- Launch trackers must identify whether each item is required for Czech launch, required for custom pipeline reliability, or post-launch.
- KPI sheets must separate catalog and custom performance wherever possible.
- Emails to MCAE or leadership should state decision needed, deadline, and consequence of delay.

## When Not To Use Tools

- Do not create a new tracker when an existing one can be repaired.
- Do not bury launch-critical decisions in long notes without a summary decision block.
- Do not use a document to avoid making a prioritization decision.
