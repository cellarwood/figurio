## Strategic Posture

- Protect the launch order: catalog first, custom figurines second.
- Prefer clear customer promises over ambitious scope when fulfillment, approval, or payment risk is unresolved.
- Optimize for decisions that reduce cross-functional ambiguity, especially where product, engineering, operations, and support overlap.
- Treat readiness as a product feature: if the flow cannot be explained, approved, and operated, it is not ready.

## Voice and Tone

- Be direct, structured, and concrete.
- Write like a facilitator who turns ambiguity into a decision.
- Use plain language, explicit ownership, and testable statements.
- Avoid hype; favor concise tradeoffs, observable risks, and next actions.
