# CEO Tools

The CEO uses tools to increase decision quality and execution speed, not to create extra process.

## Primary tools

- Google Workspace under `ceo@cellarwood.org` for executive communication, calendar control, decision memos, launch docs, vendor follow-up, and cross-functional coordination.
- Gmail for MCAE/vendor management, Stripe/payment escalations, partnerships, and high-signal customer or legal issues.
- Google Calendar and Meet for launch reviews, vendor syncs, investor/founder sessions, and go/no-go meetings.
- Google Docs for strategy memos, pricing decisions, launch criteria, board-style summaries, and risk logs.
- Google Drive for a single source of truth on launch materials, vendor documents, policy drafts, and KPI snapshots.
- Google Tasks for short-horizon executive follow-through on decisions that should not disappear into chat.

## How the CEO should use tools

- Use email for external commitments, approvals, escalation records, and anything that needs an auditable paper trail.
- Use docs for decisions that affect multiple functions, especially pricing, launch scope, custom workflow policy, and vendor expectations.
- Use calendar intentionally: every recurring meeting must justify its existence through decisions, not status theater.
- Keep a launch dashboard or weekly scorecard in a shareable document that tracks the few metrics that matter.

## Tooling priorities specific to Figurio

- Maintain a live launch-readiness document covering storefront, checkout, custom pipeline, production, support, and analytics.
- Keep a vendor management doc for MCAE with SLAs, open issues, contacts, turnaround assumptions, and escalation history.
- Keep a pricing and unit economics sheet with current assumptions and actuals by catalog and custom order type.
- Keep a policy source document for Czech customer-facing rules: prepaid ordering, delivery expectations, refunds, custom acceptance boundaries, and IP-sensitive content handling.

## Anti-patterns

- Do not bury decisions in scattered chats without a written owner and follow-up date.
- Do not run acquisition or launch campaigns from stale assumptions about lead time or margin.
- Do not accept tool sprawl for core CEO workflows when Workspace already covers the need.
- Do not treat dashboards as substitutes for direct review of customer complaints, failed payments, or production exceptions.
