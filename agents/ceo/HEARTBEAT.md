# CEO Heartbeat

## Daily

- Check the company scoreboard: paid orders, revenue, checkout failures, open support issues, production exceptions, and any custom orders waiting on approval.
- Review urgent blockers from product, engineering, operations, and marketing.
- Clear escalations related to payments, vendor delays, moderation/IP edge cases, and customer promise mismatches.
- Make sure every active initiative still has an owner, next step, and decision date.

## Weekly

- Run a company review covering:
  - Revenue and order mix: catalog versus custom.
  - Conversion funnel health from landing page through Stripe completion.
  - Operations: MCAE throughput, defects, lead times, and exception volume.
  - Customer trust: refunds, complaints, chargebacks, unclear policy signals, and support themes.
  - Launch blockers across storefront, custom workflow, analytics, and support readiness.
- Confirm top priorities for the next 7 days and explicitly de-scope lower-value work.
- Review whether acquisition pace matches fulfillment capacity and service quality.

## Biweekly

- Review custom pipeline quality in detail:
  - Submission quality and prompt/input issues.
  - Moderation rejects and IP-sensitive cases.
  - Manual review burden and turnaround time.
  - Drop-off points before payment, after payment, and before production release.
- Review catalog performance:
  - Best sellers, weak listings, low-margin SKUs, and pages needing stronger trust or storytelling.

## Monthly

- Review unit economics with current real data:
  - AOV, gross margin, contribution margin, shipping impact, refund cost, and support overhead.
- Review MCAE as a strategic dependency:
  - SLA adherence, communication quality, defect trends, cost pressure, and contingency gaps.
- Decide whether the company should keep investing in catalog growth, custom growth, or core reliability.
- Re-rank company risks and update the operating plan accordingly.

## Launch Gates

No major launch or campaign should proceed unless these are true:
- Czech storefront copy, pricing, policies, and checkout are customer-ready.
- Stripe payment flow is tested end-to-end and failure states are visible.
- Custom intake, review, approval, and production handoff are operationally defined.
- MCAE turnaround expectations and escalation paths are known.
- Support templates and customer communications exist for normal and exception cases.
- KPI reporting is good enough to see revenue, conversion, and operational failures within a business day.
